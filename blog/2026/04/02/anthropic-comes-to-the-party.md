As of yesterday I can finally announce that (along with my colleague
[Alex Potanin](https://comp.anu.edu.au/people/alex-potanin/)) I have received
$500k in Claude API credits for the students to use in COMP4020 Agentic Coding
Studio. As per the
[Anthropic press release](https://www.anthropic.com/news/australia-MOU):

> The ANU School of Computing is also embedding Claude into new courses to train
> the next generation of Australian developers and scientists.

This is a no-strings-attached gift; for us to use as we see fit in figuring out
how to train the next generation (and re-train the current one) of software
engineers in this
[new world of agentic coding](/blog/2026/03/30/comp4020-whats-the-theory-here/).
It also means that my worries about (not) being able to give my students the
[sharpest tools](/blog/2026/02/17/comp4020-sharp-tools/) is now gone. Claude
Code (and the models that power it) has been
[my](/blog/2026/02/18/ben-s-dev-setup-2026-edition/) [daily](/blog/2025/07/23/running-claude-code-within-zed/) [driver](/blog/2025/06/06/agentic-elixir-superpowers-zed-tidewave-ashai/) [for](/blog/2025/12/23/coding-agents-and-the-plasticity-of-code/) [the](/blog/2026/02/05/out-of-the-browser-on-the-power-of-interfaces-for-agentic-ai/) [last](/blog/2025/07/17/agentic-ai-llms-with-stones/) [year](/blog/2024/08/12/hosting-a-genai-trivia-night/),
and I think it's now unquestioned that they're the leader in this space.

The one remaining "infrastructure" piece of the puzzle is where to develop the
prototypes and how to put them on the open web (as per
[this post](/blog/2026/03/05/comp4020-safety-yolo-and-the-open-web/)). If you
work at a company that provides that sort of infra and want to be part of this,
then [get in touch](mailto:ben.swift@anu.edu.au).
