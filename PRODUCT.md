# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Mixed audience: academic peers and PhD students looking for publications and
research context; current or prospective students exploring courses and
technical posts; and creative/tech generalists arriving via search or social
media. The site serves all three groups without alienating any of them.

## Product Purpose

Personal website and blog for Ben Swift — academic, livecoder, and educator at
the ANU School of Cybernetics. It's the canonical home for publications,
teaching material, livecoding history, and a long-running technical blog.
Success means readers find what they came for quickly and the site's personality
registers without getting in the way of the content.

## Brand Personality

Warm, rigorous, playful. Accessible academic who doesn't take themselves too
seriously — serious ideas delivered with a light touch.

## Anti-references

Slick startup landing pages (no hero animations-as-marketing, gradient CTAs,
marketing-speak) and corporate/institutional sites (no committee-designed
blandness, stock photos, or university template aesthetic).

## Design Principles

1. **Content first** — decoration supports, never competes
2. **Warm darkness** — inviting, not cold; purple-tinted neutrals over pure
   greys
3. **Quiet confidence** — intentional and considered without drawing attention
   to itself
4. **Playful details** — small surprises that reward attention without demanding
   it
5. **Accessibility as craft** — not a checkbox but a design value

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Dark-only theme with `color-scheme: dark`. WCAG AA contrast as a floor,
reduced-motion alternatives for all animation (the WebGL hero has a static image
fallback), keyboard-reachable scrollable code blocks, skip links, and semantic
HTML throughout. Typography choices historically prioritised legibility
(Atkinson Hyperlegible heritage).
