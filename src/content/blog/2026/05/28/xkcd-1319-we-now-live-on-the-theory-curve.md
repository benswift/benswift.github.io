description:
  "XKCD 1319 set the theory and reality curves of automation against each other.
  With LLMs writing the scripts, the theory curve has won."
tags: [ai, dev]
---

For most of my career, [XKCD 1319](https://xkcd.com/1319/) has (like many XKCDs)
felt funny but uncomfortably close to the truth. But now 6 months after the
[November 2025 inflection point](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jan/4/inflection/)
Claude and friends have changed this sort of thing. I'm now regularly writing
scripts that run first time, then evaporate, then I go back to what I was
actually doing. We now live on the theory curve.

I ran a web search to check if anyone had got there first. Of course they had. A
[Hacker News commenter](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=44770505) made
exactly the point last August, complete with the gleeful framing "productivity
gains built from productivity gains." Ah well, nothing new under the sun.

The cost-benefit arithmetic of automation, the thing XKCD 1319 mocked, is baked
into how developers think about their own time. If that arithmetic has actually
changed, it's a lot of mental machinery to retire.[^caveat]

[^caveat]:
    I am aware this is exactly what someone says before discovering they were on
    the reality curve all along. We'll see.
