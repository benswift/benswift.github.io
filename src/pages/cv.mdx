## Research interests

- AI education and demystification---making the mechanics of language models
  legible through hands-on, unplugged pedagogy
- agentic AI workflows and their implications for software development practice
- human-AI creative systems, from livecoded music to adversarial art
- critical perspectives on machine learning---explainability, governance, and
  the politics of automation
- livecoding and computational art

## Selected projects

These are active research-through-practice projects from the Cybernetic Studio
at the ANU School of Cybernetics.

- **[LLMs Unplugged](https://llmsunplugged.org)** --- hands-on teaching
  resources where learners build language models with pen, paper, and dice.
  Delivered to 400+ participants across secondary, tertiary, and executive
  education contexts. Published at ACE 2026.
- **VLM Perception Experiments** --- controlled experiments testing whether
  vision language models can determine occlusion order in visual scenes, with
  statistical analysis across multiple frontier models.
- **APS AI Transparency Tracker** --- automated monitoring of AI Transparency
  Statements published by Australian Government departments, tracking how AI
  governance policies evolve over time.
- **LLM Brainscan** --- visualises transformer weight changes during training on
  an 8K display, one pixel per parameter.
- **Perceptron Apparatus** --- a 1.2-metre diameter physical device that
  performs artificial neural network calculations by hand. Launched at SXSW
  Sydney 2025.
- **[COMP4020: Agentic Coding Studio](https://comp.anu.edu.au/study/special-topics/)**
  --- new ANU course (S2 2026) on LLM-agent-driven software development
  workflows, moving beyond naive vibecoding to a studio-based iterate-and-test
  approach.
