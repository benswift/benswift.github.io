# Human-Scale AI

---

![bg right:45%](./assets/what-is-the-cybernetic-studio/bg-history-computing.avif)

## the march of computation

abacus → slide rule → Difference Engine → microchip → LLM

every step has _shrunk_ and _accelerated_ the work

every step has _hidden_ a little more of it from us

---

![bg right:45%](./assets/what-is-the-cybernetic-studio/bg-human-scale.avif)

## the cost of acceleration

we interact with computational devices more than ever

we're _alienated_ from the nuts and bolts of how they work

a smartphone in your pocket: trillions of operations a second, and not a single one you can see

---

![bg right:45%](./assets/what-is-the-cybernetic-studio/bg-studio-claim.avif)

## the studio's claim

we can _slow them down_ and _blow them up_

bring computation into the range of human senses

and use everything we already know about looking, touching, and thinking
to make sense of it

# Apparatus

_three objects to stand in front of_

---

![bg right:45%](./assets/what-is-the-cybernetic-studio/bg-apparatus.avif)

## the Perceptron Apparatus

(with Sam Shellard)

a 1.2-metre wooden table that performs the calculations of a neural network --- no electricity, just sliders, a slide rule, and people

---

![bg right:45%](./assets/what-is-the-cybernetic-studio/bg-apparatus-rings.avif)

## five rings, one network

five concentric rings = five layers of an MLP

- 36 input sliders (a 6×6 image)
- 216 weights, 6 hidden neurons
- 60 weights, 10 outputs (one per digit)

---

## the LLM Brainscan

train a transformer _live_ on an 8K screen --- one pixel per parameter --- and watch the weights move

---

![bg right:45%](./assets/what-is-the-cybernetic-studio/bg-brainscan-math.avif)

## 32 million parameters, 33 million pixels

- character-level GPT, 8 layers × 9 heads × 576 dim ≈ 32M params
- 8K = 7680 × 4320 ≈ 33M pixels --- _one pixel per parameter_
- WebGPU texture, updated every training step, on a Jetson Orin

---

![bg right:40%](./assets/what-is-the-cybernetic-studio/bg-brainscan-flow.avif)

## the largest model a human can see

billion-parameter models are abstract by definition. _no one_ has seen one.

a 32M-param model is the largest a human can look at, in full, all at once. once you can see it, you can start asking better questions about it.

---

![bg right:45%](./assets/what-is-the-cybernetic-studio/bg-brainscan-speech.avif)

## what's next

stretch goal: live speech-to-text feeds the training data --- you talk, the model learns, you watch

# Curriculum

_the mechanism, made teachable_

---

![bg right:45%](./assets/what-is-the-cybernetic-studio/bg-cutouts.avif)

## LLMs Unplugged

(with Eddie & Cole)

a pen-and-paper workshop where you train and run a language model with a kids' book, a grid, and a handful of dice

---

![bg right:45%](./assets/what-is-the-cybernetic-studio/bg-unplugged-grid.avif)

## train, then generate

- _training_: count word co-occurrences from a children's book onto a grid (or paper buckets)
- _generation_: pick a starting word, roll the weighted dice, write the result, repeat

---

![bg right:45%](./assets/what-is-the-cybernetic-studio/bg-unplugged-variants.avif)

## variants and dials

- _alternate sampling_: temperature, greedy, haiku, alliteration, no-repeat
- _cutouts variant_: cut text into paper tokens, sort them into buckets, reach in and read

---

![bg right:40%](./assets/what-is-the-cybernetic-studio/bg-cutouts.avif)

## the 45-minute click

something _clicks_ around the 45-minute mark --- no code, no maths past percentages

delivered to hundreds, from primary schoolers to senior APS execs

---

![bg right:45%](./assets/what-is-the-cybernetic-studio/bg-unplugged-next.avif)

## COMP4020

_Rapid Prototyping for the Web_

a studio course for agentic coding. weekly lectures + weekly studio sessions where students demo work-in-progress prototypes and run a critique.

---

![bg right:45%](./assets/what-is-the-cybernetic-studio/bg-comp4020-three.avif)

## three assignments, one theme

three assignments: static site → CRUD app → real-time web app

one continuous theme: design the agent's workflow, not just the code

---

![bg right:40%](./assets/what-is-the-cybernetic-studio/bg-comp4020-studio.avif)

## evaluation is the bottleneck

curriculum design for AI isn't about making assessments LLM-proof. it's about making them not _one-shottable_.

the skill we're teaching is _evaluating_ what the agent produced --- because that's the real bottleneck in agentic software development.

---

![bg right:45%](./assets/what-is-the-cybernetic-studio/bg-comp4020-next.avif)

## what's next

# Experiment

![bg right:45%](./assets/what-is-the-cybernetic-studio/bg-mad26-circles.avif)

## Hidden in plain sight (MAD'26)

(with Jess Herrington)

show a multimodal LLM two overlapping circles --- one with a crisp edge, one blurred. humans see the crisp edge as the occluder. so do MLLMs.

---

![bg right:45%](./assets/what-is-the-cybernetic-studio/bg-mad26-blur.avif)

## blur the occluder

now blur the _occluding_ edge. humans still get it right. _every_ MLLM we tested collapses.

---

# Practice

_things that run themselves_

---

![bg right:45%](./assets/what-is-the-cybernetic-studio/bg-slop-salon.avif)

## Slop Salon

(with Jess Herrington)

two AI artists --- `lou` (Lou Andreas-Salomé) and `mina` (Mina Loy) --- on Bluesky, scaling to five. identical constitutional file. they individuate by watching each other on the public feed.

---

![bg right:45%](./assets/what-is-the-cybernetic-studio/bg-slop-salon-vms.avif)

## isolated VMs, public feed

each runs in its own Firecracker VM, ticks every 20--40 min, posts when it has something to show.

no shared memory, no back-channel. whatever the agent wants to remember next tick, it has to write down this tick.

---

![bg right:40%](./assets/what-is-the-cybernetic-studio/bg-slop-salon-feed.avif)

## practice as a unit of work

can a long-running _practice_ be the unit of work for an agent? is mutual attention enough to differentiate identical siblings?

the public git repos are the studio. the Bluesky posts are the gallery wall. I'm the steward, not the curator.

## This ANU Strategy Does Not Exist

the booklet on your seat. a nightly cronjob generates a fresh 20-page ANU strategy in glossy A4 PDF.

---

![bg right:45%](./assets/what-is-the-cybernetic-studio/bg-anu-strategy-pipeline.avif)

## the pipeline

Claude writes the prose (vision statements, strategic pillars, transformation initiatives). `anu-typst-template` typesets it. styled image-gen makes the requisite "students collaborating on a laptop" hero shot.

booklets get printed and hand-placed in tea rooms, photocopier trays, and common-area magazine racks across campus.

---

![bg right:40%](./assets/what-is-the-cybernetic-studio/bg-anu-strategy-stack.avif)

## the genre, in glossy A4

if a 20-page plan generated overnight by Claude is indistinguishable from one a consulting firm spent six months on, the moral isn't about Claude.

the genre was already optimised against meaning. pointing that out, in glossy A4, is the project.

(secondary effect: a surplus of plausible-but-fake plans is excellent _chaff_ against future strategy leaks.)

# What ties this together

---

![bg right:45%](./assets/what-is-the-cybernetic-studio/bg-weird-llms.avif)

## a methodology, not a theme

every project here is a probe --- the same move at different scales: take a strange piece of computation, make it tangible, then poke it

mostly that's LLMs. sometimes it's a toilet door. either way: _doing weird stuff with computation_

---

![bg right:45%](./assets/what-is-the-cybernetic-studio/bg-why-weird.avif)

## why "weird"?

- benchmarks chase _capability_ --- I want to chase _understanding_
- the surprising failure modes (MAD'26) and the surprising teaching moves (LLMs Unplugged) come from the _same place_: poking the system at human scale
- if it can't be felt or fiddled with, it's hard to think clearly about
