# Human-Scale AI

---

![bg right:45%](./assets/cybernetic-studio-show-tell/bg-history-computing.avif)

## the march of computation

abacus → slide rule → Difference Engine → microchip → LLM

every step has _shrunk_ and _accelerated_ the work

every step has _hidden_ a little more of it from us

---

![bg right:45%](./assets/cybernetic-studio-show-tell/bg-human-scale.avif)

## the cost of acceleration

we interact with computational devices more than ever

we're _alienated_ from the nuts and bolts of how they work

a smartphone in your pocket: trillions of operations a second, and not a single one you can see

---

## the studio's claim

we can _slow them down_ and _blow them up_

bring computation into the range of human senses

and use everything we already know about looking, touching, and thinking
to make sense of it

# The Perceptron Apparatus

(with Sam Shellard)

---

![bg right:45%](./assets/cybernetic-studio-show-tell/bg-apparatus.avif)

## the pitch

a 1.2-metre wooden table that performs the calculations of a neural network --- no electricity, just sliders, a slide rule, and people

---

## key questions

- what does AI _look_ like if you build it without electronics?
- what changes when computation has to be done _in public_, _by hand_?
- if the network is intelligent, where does that intelligence _live_?

---

_apparatus in use --- people gathered around the table, sliders mid-shift_

---

![bg right:50%](./assets/cybernetic-studio-show-tell/bg-apparatus-dial.avif)

## how it works

five concentric rings = five layers of an MLP

- ring A: 36 input sliders (a 6×6 image)
- ring B: 216 weight sliders
- ring C: 6 hidden activations
- ring D: 60 weight sliders
- ring E: 10 output sliders (one per digit)

multiply with the outer slide rule. accumulate by moving sliders. apply ReLU. argmax wins.

# Hidden in plain sight

(MAD'26 with Jess Herrington)

---

![bg right:45%](./assets/cybernetic-studio-show-tell/bg-mad26-circles.avif)

## the pitch

multimodal LLMs have a _perceptual blind spot_ --- they fail at a depth cue any human solves effortlessly

---

## key questions

- do multimodal LLMs really _see_, or do they reason about a verbal description of what they see?
- if they harbour systematic perceptual blind spots, what does that mean for using them to detect manipulated content?

---

{/* _class: impact */}

# (photo here)

_the actual experimental stimuli --- real circles, real crisp/blurred edges_

---

![bg right:45%](./assets/cybernetic-studio-show-tell/bg-mad26-bias.avif)

## how it works

show the model two overlapping circles. one has a crisp edge, one is blurred.

humans see the _crisp_ edge as the occluder --- the thing in front. so do MLLMs.

now blur the _occluding_ edge. humans still get it right. MLLMs collapse.

---

{/* _class: impact */}

# 93.6% → 22.7%

_blur the wrong edge, every MLLM we tested falls over_

---

## what else we found

- six MLLMs across two providers --- _every_ one shows the bias
- "thinking" mode helps OpenAI, hurts Anthropic

---

![bg right:40%](./assets/cybernetic-studio-show-tell/bg-mad26-circles.avif)

## why you should care

these models are already being deployed in disinformation pipelines

a one-line photoshop trick (blur the wrong edge) can make a manipulated image read "untampered" to every model we tested

the bias is not in the eyes --- it's in the words the model uses to reason about the eyes

# LLMs Unplugged

(with Eddie & Cole)

---

![bg right:45%](./assets/cybernetic-studio-show-tell/bg-cutouts.avif)

## the pitch

a pen-and-paper workshop where you train and run a language model with a kids' book, a grid, and a handful of dice

---

## key questions

- can you really demystify ChatGPT without _any_ hand-waving?
- what clicks when you do the maths yourself, slowly, in a room with other people?
- what's the smallest possible language model that's still recognisably _the same kind of thing_?

---

{/* _class: impact */}

# (photo here)

_workshop in progress --- participants writing tallies on a grid, dice on the table_

---

![bg right:45%](./assets/cybernetic-studio-show-tell/bg-cutouts.avif)

## how it works

phase 1 --- _training_: count word co-occurrences from a children's book onto a grid (or into paper buckets)

phase 2 --- _generation_: pick a starting word, roll the weighted dice, write the result, repeat

phase 3 --- _alternate sampling_: temperature, greedy, haiku, alliteration, no-repeat...

phase 4 --- _scale up_: pre-printed booklets that contain larger pre-trained models

---

## the new variant --- _cutouts_

instead of tally marks on a grid, _cut up_ the text into paper tokens and sort them into buckets

generation = reach into a bucket, pull out a paper token, read it

same maths. completely different feel.

---

![bg right:40%](./assets/cybernetic-studio-show-tell/bg-cutouts.avif)

## why you should care

- delivered to hundreds, from primary schoolers to senior APS execs
- something _clicks_ around the 45-minute mark --- no code, no maths past percentages
- SXSW '25 panel, ACE'26 paper

# LLM Brainscan

---

![bg right:45%](./assets/cybernetic-studio-show-tell/bg-brainscan-pixels.avif)

## the pitch

train a transformer _live_ on an 8K screen --- one pixel per parameter --- and watch the weights move

---

## key questions

- what does training _look_ like if you can see every weight at once?
- is there visual structure in a learning model that you'd never notice in a loss curve?
- what kind of model is the right size to be _the most data-rich artefact you've ever seen_?

---

# (photo here)

_the brainscan running live --- 8K screen alive with shifting weights_

---

![bg right:50%](./assets/cybernetic-studio-show-tell/bg-brainscan-flow.avif)

## how it works

- character-level GPT, 8 layers × 9 heads × 576 dim ≈ 32M params
- 8K = 7680 × 4320 ≈ 33M pixels --- _one pixel per parameter_
- WebGPU texture, updated every training step
- runs on a Jetson Orin (slow is fine; we want to _see_ it)

stretch goal: live speech-to-text feeding the training data --- you talk, the model learns, you watch

---

![bg right:40%](./assets/cybernetic-studio-show-tell/bg-brainscan-pixels.avif)

## why you should care

billion-parameter models are abstract by definition. _no one_ has seen one.

a 32M-param model is the largest model a human can look at, in full, all at once

and once you can see it, you can start asking better questions about it

# What ties this together

---

![bg right:45%](./assets/cybernetic-studio-show-tell/bg-weird-llms.avif)

## a methodology, not a theme

every project here is a probe --- the same move at different scales: take a strange piece of computation, make it tangible, then poke it

call it what it is --- _doing weird stuff with LLMs_

---

## why "weird"?

- benchmarks chase _capability_ --- I want to chase _understanding_
- the surprising failure modes (MAD'26) and the surprising teaching moves (LLMs Unplugged) come from the _same place_: poking the system at human scale
- if it can't be felt or fiddled with, it's hard to think clearly about
