## Applying cybernetic approaches to law reform

- legal doctrinal methodology dominates law reform; few lawyers were involved in early cybernetics, and there's been little uptake since
- the question: can tools from cybernetics be useful in the law reform context? A practical focus
- national security law as the worked example
- (topic has evolved since TPR; part-time candidate, working full-time)

---

## PhD by compilation — four published, plus WIP

- powers and functions of national security agencies
- national security legislation, administrative law and AI
- ASIO interception and data access powers
- modernising the system for issuing warrants — findings from the SLAID Act review
- **WIP:** how secrecy affects regulation; reviewing NS laws and the role of civil society engagement
- annexures: four law-reform reports — secrecy offences, special cyber-warrants, espionage, defining terrorism

---

## Still to do

- **2026** — one more article to publication
- **2027** (planning to go full-time on the PhD)
  - introduction and overview
  - legal doctrinal methodology vs cybernetic approaches
  - linking sections for each publication
  - overall conclusions and impact

---

## Surviving a PhD while working full-time

- great supervisors
- leverage PhD research for work, and work for the PhD
- PhD by compilation — bite-sized pieces, so you can see and show progress
- be disciplined with time and effort
- won't be the right path for everyone

## To what extent can AI replicate Indigenous worldviews? · 12:20 · 5 min

## The challenge

- AI is transforming health, education, security and governance
- Indigenous knowledge is relational, spiritual and place-based
- AI predicts patterns rather than lived relationships
- can AI authentically replicate Indigenous worldviews?

---

## Research design

- comparative study across Māori, Aboriginal, Torres Strait Islander and Pacific knowledge systems
- methods: Kaupapa Māori, Yarning, Talanoa, AI audits and interviews
- cybernetics examines relationships between people, technology and governance
- develop an Indigenous-led AI governance framework

---

## Expected impact

- protect Indigenous Data Sovereignty
- support ethical AI policy
- strengthen digital trust and cultural integrity
- inform governments, industry and communities

---

![bg contain](./assets/gareth-rauora-example.avif)

---

## An example

- an AI-generated Māori game character, released without meaningful Māori consultation --- cultural inaccuracies and associations that caused significant concern
- AI can reproduce Indigenous identities without understanding whakapapa, tikanga or cultural context
- highlights the need for Indigenous governance, cultural oversight and authentic engagement in AI-enabled technologies
- illustrates the central question: can AI replicate Indigenous worldviews, or only imitate their visible features?

---

## Kia ora

- builds on previous PhD and leadership in Indigenous health, national security and AI
- brings together AI, cybernetics, governance and Indigenous knowledge
- deliverable: an Indigenous AI Authenticity & Integrity Framework
- future: Indigenous peoples shaping AI

# Thomas McEvoy Zafiropulos

## Art and the divided self · 12:30 · 5 min

{/* notes: Ported to house style from Thomas's single ODP slide (email 17 July) — split into two slides for legibility. */}

---

## A cybernetics of ontological repair

- many contemporary philosophers and cognitive scientists now understand the self as a process rather than a static substance --- **if so, can it also be understood as a viable cybernetic system?**
- how does the self-system emerge, and how is it maintained?
- how and why does it break down?
- what role does art play in maintaining or restoring viability?

---

## Year one

- **theory:** develop a cybernetic account of conceptual engineering --- what changes when concepts are understood as processes rather than static objects?
- **literature:** examine historical systems-based accounts of psychopathology and develop a cybernetic etiology of psychopathology
- **empirical groundwork:** analyse how different disciplines conceptualise "the self" using word-embedding methods
- **pilot intervention:** design an art-based intervention to test the role of aesthetic experience in self-viability
